What is Linkstash?

Your web browser lets you collect links to websites as bookmarks so you don't have to remember them all. When you login to your web browser, your bookmarks follow you from machine to machine. That's pretty convenient.

You can group those links into folders on the bookmarks bar to better organize them. It's not a terrible system, but it's based on the user experience of your computer's filesystem. It's very familiar. But, we asked the question, "Can it be done better?"

What's Different about Linkstash?

Linkstash treats your bookmarks more like items in a catalogue. You can:

  • rate and review links individually, or as groups of links called collections
  • leave comments on your links or collections as notes to yourself, or warnings to others
  • assign categories to your links to help yourself and others find those links across the site, across groups, and across users
  • make your collections private, while still exploring public collections to integrate discovered links into your own collections

Linkstash is more than a link management tool, though. At its heart, it's a social media site, where you can connect with other users that share your interests.
